A Field Guide to
Neurodivergent Constellations

Eight patterns in the sky, renamed for what they actually are. Each one forged in the same stellar furnace as your bones. Each one a different way of being made of stars.

L★S
How to use this guide Each entry gives the traditional name (what the old charts called it), the reclaimed name, L★S coordinates, brief field notes, and a full observing record. Click any entry to open its full notes.
On renaming The old names belong to old frameworks — frameworks that measured the sky's variation as error. These names belong to the paradigm that doesn't pathologize its own variation.
On the diagrams Each diagram shows the actual star positions of the traditional constellation, redrawn with the new pattern made visible. The stars haven't moved. Only the story has changed.

All constellations are made of the same material — stellar ash, scattered by supernovae, gathered by gravity into the improbable configurations we call bodies and minds. The sky doesn't rank its patterns. Neither do we.
