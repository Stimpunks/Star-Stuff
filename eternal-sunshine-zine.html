The nearest star

Every warm morning
you have ever felt
is starlight

The Sun is a star. The nearest one. The warmth you feel on your face is not like starlight — it is starlight, crossing ninety-three million miles of empty space to land on skin made of older stars.

That light left the Sun's surface about eight minutes and twenty seconds ago. But before that, it spent an unimaginably long time — tens of thousands of years — working its way out from the core, where fusion had just made it, colliding and scattering the whole way up.

So the warmth arriving now began its journey before there were cities, before there was writing. Eternal sunshine is eternal starlight. It always was.

Eternal Sunshine · Stimpunks 3
Brighter days · built together

You were never dim.
The sky was.

Ariana Grande named an album Eternal Sunshine and a foundation Brighter Days Ahead. We heard our own language in both. Then that foundation reached toward us — the Brighter Days Ahead Foundation is supporting Stimpunks, one of the small, grassroots organizations it funds to do care work for communities the world treats as disposable. We recognize that work. It is our work.

And we hear the counter-deficit truth folded into the title. The light is already in you. It always was. Brighter days are not a repair — they are better conditions for light that was always there.

You were never dim. The sky was. We are here to clear it.

Because how you're funded says whether you're believed

To be seen and funded this way — championed, not pitied; trusted, not tested — is rare. No forms to prove worth, no temporal lottery to win. Trust-based giving funds the people closest to the need and cuts the burden, the way disabled communities have practiced all along: nothing about us without us.

Notshine brighter and you'll be worthy. The light was never in question.
Notpositivity instead of change. Clearing the sky means changing conditions, not moods.
Notgratitude as the price of support. Being funded should not cost you your dignity.
Notinspiration porn. You are not a lesson for anyone else's comfort.

Authenticity is our eternal sunshine, and brighter days are built together. ★☀️

Reflection

When have you been treated as already-light — championed and trusted — rather than as something to fix? What did that make possible?

If you were never dim, what is the sky in your life — the conditions, not the person — that could be cleared, and who would help clear it?

Eternal Sunshine is the sixth zine in the Stimpunks series, adapted from the "Eternal Sunshine" page and the essay "Ariana Grande, Brighter Days Ahead, Stimpunks, and the Future of Philanthropy." It is offered freely, to be printed and folded and handed to someone who needs it.

The epigraph adapts Carl Sagan's "We are star stuff harvesting sunlight" (Cosmos, 1980) — the ★stuff reading is "starlight." The counter-deficit and trust-based-giving arguments draw on Lydia X.Z. Brown on philanthropy's ableism, Liz Jackson and Alex Haagaard on "temporal lotteries," and the disability-rights principle "nothing about us without us" (Ed Roberts; the South African disability movement). References to Ariana Grande and the Brighter Days Ahead Foundation are factual; the counter-deficit reading is ours. Star Stuff artwork by Rainbow Mosho ("Where We Come From," MoonMars Museum).
