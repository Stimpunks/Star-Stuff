A Field Guide to
Worlds That Work by Other Rules

Eight worlds in our own solar system that run on rules Earth would call impossible — and thrive on them. Not failed Earths. Other ways for a world to be.

L★S
How to use this guide Each entry gives the world, the Earth-rule it breaks, the rule it runs on instead, the result, key data, and full field notes. Click any entry to open its notes.
On "other rules" "Impossible" almost always means "impossible by Earth's defaults." These worlds aren't failed Earths; they run on other physics, other chemistries, other orientations — and thrive. Different rules, not broken rules.
On the diagrams Each diagram is the world itself, drawn with the feature that defines it — an ocean under ice, a sideways tilt, a plume to space. Same frame each time; each world unmistakably its own.

"Impossible" is almost always short for "impossible here, by our rules." Every world in this guide keeps running on some other rule — and the sky is full of them. Different rules, not broken rules. (Figures are approximate, and some — like the depth of Europa's ocean — are still being measured.)
